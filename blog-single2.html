Glass Screen Printing: Familiar with the 8 Techniques

Glass Screen Printing: Familiar with the 8 Techniques

  • China Glass Network
  • Feb 15, 2020

  1. Today's glass etching screen printing techniques have made etching simpler, more convenient, and time-saving. Glass frosting decoration screen printing method involves first screen printing a colored or colorless glass flux layer (fluxing agent) on the glass surface, followed by sprinkling glass frost particles onto this layer of glass flux. This layer is then fused at temperatures ranging from 500°C to 590°C to create a raised effect. If colored flux is screen printed on the glass, and the frost is transparent, high-temperature fusion results in the colored flux layer fading at the frost pattern area, leaving behind a colored, elevated transparent relief pattern on the glass surface.

  2. Glass Frost Pattern Screen Printing Method

Frost patterns, commonly known as 'orange peel patterns,' consist of tiny low-melting-point glass particles. These small glass particles, with high lead content, come in both colored and colorless variants, including red, yellow, blue, green, white, and intermediate shades. Frost pattern decoration through screen printing is elegant and graceful, often used in architectural and artistic glass decoration, such as upscale glassware and lamp ornamentation.

The glass frost decoration screen printing method involves first screen printing a colored or colorless glass flux layer on the glass surface, then sprinkling frost glass particles onto this layer, followed by firing at temperatures ranging from 500°C to 590°C. This fusing process creates a relief effect between the glass surface's flux layer and the frost particle layer. If colored flux is used and the frost is transparent, the fusion causes the flux layer beneath the frost pattern area to lose color, resulting in a colored, raised transparent relief pattern.

  1. Glass Sandblasting Screen Printing Method

Sandblasting involves adhering a specific size area of glass enamel powder to the glass surface, followed by high-temperature firing between 580°C and 600°C. This method creates a decorative effect with a color distinct from the glass base. Adhering glass enamel powder can be done with a pen or a roller. Sandblasting screen printing can be used to create hollow patterns on the sandblasted surface. This is achieved by first screen printing a pattern using a resist flux on the glass product's surface. After drying, sandblasting is performed, followed by high-temperature firing. The sandblasted areas melt onto the glass surface during firing, revealing a transparent hollow pattern against the translucent sandblasted surface.

  1. Glass Ion Exchange Coloring via Screen Printing

This method involves exchanging sodium ions in glass with silver ions, reducing them to metallic colloids to achieve colored glass. Silver paste containing silver ions is screen printed onto the desired colored areas of a transparent glass sheet. After firing and cleaning, the printed areas change from yellow to brown. The screen printing ink for this technique typically includes silver carbonate, copper sulfate (burnt powder), iron dioxide, talcum powder, and a small amount of zinc powder as a catalyst.

  1. Glass Touch Control Panel Screen Printing

In modern designs for appliances like microwave ovens, microcomputer-integrated touch controls are used for temperature adjustments and switches. These touch controls are printed onto glass panels and have no moving parts or bumps, providing a smooth, easily cleanable surface that responds to touch.

The touch control panel surface is covered with a conductive film and is decorated and displays text using screen-printed ceramic inks. It boasts mechanical wear resistance, chemical corrosion resistance, and ease of cleaning. The glass can be tinted by screen printing organic coatings on the backside, which are highly durable and provide versatility in ink selection.

  1. Glass Matte Surface Screen Printing

This method involves screen printing a matte ink on the glass surface, which gives it a tea-colored appearance after a few minutes of washing. Unlike etching, which uses hydrofluoric acid and can be dangerous, this method uses an acid-free ink, making it simpler to operate while achieving a similar matte effect.

  1. Electrically Heated Defrosting Line Screen Printing

To prevent condensation on car rear windows, electrically heated defrosting lines are increasingly utilized. This technique involves applying silver paste as lines on smooth glass, and then firing while bending the glass. When rear windows become foggy, the lines heat up through electrical current, raising the glass surface temperature to clear away moisture.

In some cars, automatic defrosting is achieved not through manual switches but through automatic power-on and power-off mechanisms. These automatic systems use sensors with serrated electrodes screen printed on thin glass sheets. A humidity-sensitive layer covers the electrodes, allowing automatic control of power-on and power-off based on humidity changes.

  1. Liquid Crystal Display Module Screen Printing

Liquid crystal numeric display modules consist of two thin glass plates sealed around the edges, enclosing liquid crystal material and forming a digital display. The screen printing technique for these modules involves printing transparent, electrically conductive traces in the shape of the number '8' on the glass surfaces. This '8' is made up of seven parts, and it can be used to partially increase the voltage, displaying the corresponding number. Creating this '8' pattern involves using acid-resistant ink to screen print the entire conductive trace onto the glass, followed by acid etching to remove the unprinted areas, leaving behind the desired '8' pattern.
