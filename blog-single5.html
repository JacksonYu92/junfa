The Structure of Photovoltaic Glass and the Unique Advantages of Ultra Clear Glass

The Structure of Photovoltaic Glass and the Unique Advantages of Ultra Clear Glass

  • China Glass Network
  • Jan 04, 2019

With the emergence of float glass technology, mass production of high-quality, flat, and uniform glass has become possible, enriching the variety of processed glass. In addition to commonly used high-strength tempered glass, there are also types such as frosted glass and dimming glass that focus on privacy protection, laminated glass with enhanced bulletproof safety, and wired glass. In this article, we will mainly introduce the structure of photovoltaic glass and explain the unique advantages of ultra-clear photovoltaic glass.

1. Structure of Photovoltaic Glass

Solar photovoltaic glass is a special glass that incorporates solar cells through lamination, enabling the conversion of solar radiation into electricity. It includes related current output devices and cables. The structure consists of low-iron glass, solar cells, film, back glass, and special metal conductors. The solar cells are sealed between a piece of low-iron glass and a back glass using film. This unique type of glass is used for generating electricity from solar radiation and is a novel high-tech glass product for construction purposes. Using low-iron glass to cover solar cells ensures high solar light transmittance. Tempered low-iron glass has enhanced resistance to wind pressure and can withstand large temperature variations between day and night.

It is widely used in building facades, photovoltaic roofs, sun shading, solar power generation systems, and many other fields. Installation can be done using visible frame, hidden frame, or various types of profiles in combination with curtain walls.

2. Unique Advantages of Ultra Clear Glass

  1. Low Spontaneous Breakage Rate: Ultra-clear glass effectively protects cultural relics. It is made from high-purity raw materials and does not contain various impurities that can cause spontaneous breakage in tempered glass. This significantly reduces the rate of self-explosion after tempering.

  2. Consistent Color: Due to mature technology and leading technical blocking, ultra-clear glass uses advanced color analysis instruments and software, ensuring consistent glass color.

  3. High Visible Light Transmittance and Good Transparency: Ultra-clear glass offers over 91.5% visible light transmittance, providing crystal-clear quality that makes displayed items more vivid and highlights their true appearance.

  4. Low Ultraviolet Transmittance: Compared to other glasses, ultra-clear glass effectively slows down the fading and aging of various exhibited items in display cabinets. This effect is particularly pronounced in the protection of cultural relics.

  5. Large Market, High Technical Content, Strong Profitability: Ultra-clear glass has high technological content and is challenging to produce, making it highly profitable. Its high quality determines its relatively high price (some experts estimate it can be 6 to 10 times higher than regular glass), and its cost is only 2 to 3 times that of ordinary glass, resulting in higher added value.
